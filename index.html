In the oldest religion of the world, Hinduism, the cow is thought to be sacred, or deeply respected.
Hindus do not worship cows, although they are held in high esteem. The reason has to do with the cow's agricultural uses and gentle nature.
Hindus rely heavily on cows for dairy products, for tilling fields, and for dung as a source of fuel and fertilizer. So, the cow is seen as a 'caretaker' or maternal figure.
